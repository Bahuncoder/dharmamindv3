 Book I : Bala Kanda - Book Of Youth
Chapter[Sarga] 5

Introduction
In this fifth chapter of Bala Kanda, Book of Youth, we enter the capital of the Emperor Dasaratha.  The pomp and glory of his capital is depicted in this chapter.

Verse Locator
sarvaa puurvamiyaM yeshhaamaasiitkR^itsnaa vasuMdharaa |
prajaapatimupaadaaya nR^ipaaNaaM jayashaalinaam ||1-5-1
1. sarvaa puurvam=  earlier to all (once upon a time), iyaM kR^itsnaa vasuMdharaa = this entire earth, aasiit = had , jayashaalinaam yeshhaaM nR^ipaaNaam = (belonged to) those kings who were victorious,  prajaapatim upaadaaya = starting from Vaivaswata Manu (a lord of created beings)

Once upon a time, this entire earth belonged to those kings who were victorious, starting from Vaivaswata Manu, a lord of all created beings.

Comment: This stanza is to be read with the following two for it's completeness. These stanzas in this canto are deemed to be in first person as has been uttered by Valmiki. However, quotes are not included to each stanza or its meaning.

Verse Locator

4.varta ishhyaami = I am wishing to spread (all over the world), sarvam = all of, tat  idam = this Ramayana with all that content,   nikhila = everything, aaditaH = from beginning, dharma = virtue, artha = wealth, kaama = desire, sahitam = containing, shrotavyam = may be listened, anasuuyayaa = without displeasure (or) jealousy.

The rooftops [vimana] of seven-storied buildings touching the skies, is a sight to be seen.

19. suniveshita = (She) had well arranged, veshmaantaam = houses, vimaanamiva = like aeroplanes, adhigataam = which were obtained, siddhaanaam = by Siddhas, tapasaa = by meditation, divi = (in) heaven, narottama samaavR^itaam = (and which were)  peopled by the best among men.

Verse Locator
ye cha baaNairn vidhyanti viviktamaparaaparam |
shabdavedhyaM cha vitataM laghuhastaa vishaaradaaH || 1-5-20

20, 21, 22. vishaaradaaH = skilled ones, laghuhastaaH = with adept hands, ye = those who, na vidhyanti = do not hit, baaNaiH = with arrows, viviktam = someone who is alone, aparaaparam = an orphan, shabdavedyam = (or) someone easy to kill based on sound, vitatam cha = and one who is escaping, hantaaraH = (but) those who kill, nardataam = roaring, mattanaam = rutting, siMha vyaaghra varaahaaNaam = lions, bears, wild boars, vane = in a forest, balaat = with the power of, nishtai =  sharp, shastraiH = weapons,  baahubalairapi = and with the strength of hands, raajaa dasharathaH = King Dasaratha, tadaa = then, aavasayaamaasa = lived in, taam puriim = that city of Ayodhya, abhipuurNaam = filled with, sahasraiH = thousands of, taadR^ishaanaam = that kind of, mahaarathaiH = great warriors.

This city is full of skillful archers and with their dexterous hands they will not kill one who is alone, one that does not have either a predecessor or a successor in his family, a fleeing one, or by listening to the sound of the target [sono-archery]. The roaring lions, tigers and wild boars fattened for a kill, will be killed by themight of their sharp weaponry or with their mighty arms alone. The people of Ayodhya are thus benevolent and courageous, as well. That city Ayodhya, which is flooded with great and efficient warriors and heroes as described above, is made his dwelling place, by King Dasaratha.

Comment: Dasaratha in his youth, when he was once on a hunting spree, killed the son of a hermit who was drawing water from a river. Dasaratha mistook that sound to be a tiger and swung his arrow that way killing the boy. Thereby the father of the boy being a sage of eminence cursed Dasaratha to die lamenting for his son, which happens after the exile of Rama. Perhaps the sono-archery may thus have been banned in Ayodhya, lest the sad incident may not recur.

sahasradaiH satyaratairmahaatmabhi |
rmaharshhikalpairR^ishhibhishcha kevalaiH || 1-5-23
23. taam = that city of Ayodydha, aavR^itaaM = was encompassed by, agnimadbhiH = those who maintain sacrificial fires, gunavadbhiH = those who are virtuous,  dvijottamaiH = best among dvijas (Brahmins, Vaisyas, Kshatriyas), veda shhaDN^ga paaragaiH = scholars in Veda and its ancillaries , sahasradaiH = those who give away thousands, satya rataiH = those who delight in maintaining truthhood , mahaatmabhiH = and great souled ones, maharshhi kalpaiH = equal in virtue and wisdom to great saints, kevaliaH rishhibhiH cha = and also the great sages themselves.

Vedic scholars who are virtuous ones, and who are always with their ritual fires encompass Ayodhya. They are scholars in all four Vedas and in their ancillary subjects. There are great souls who donate thousands and they take delight in truthfulness. There are also people who are equal to saints and the sages themselves also live there. Thus the population of Ayodhya includes scholars, heroes, donors, traders and all of them are virtuous people. Hence, King Dasaratha made that city as his capital to rule his vast kingdom, named Kosala.

Thus this is the fifth chapter in Bala Kanda [Book I] of Valmiki Ramayana, the First Epic of India.
